Design Process

Overview

Association football, informally known as football, is more than simply a sport; it is a global phenomenon that weaves its way across nations. Its multidimensional storyline includes rich history, rules, and world-renowned tournaments, resulting in a deep and solid content structure suitable for a large web platform. To present this vibrant array of information in a seamless manner, the University of Ottawa website is used as a model, which has a user-centric design, modern look, and structural efficiency, serving as an appropriate framework for restructuring and representing the comprehensive content extracted from the Wikipedia article on Association Football.

The primary goal of combining the dense, informative content of the Wikipedia article with the structured, user-friendly design of the University of Ottawa's website is to create a web platform that is not only a repository of football but also an intuitive, navigable experience for users of various demographics. This project aims to use the design framework of the University of Ottawa website to curate an information-dense platform, ensuring that the vast history, rules, and cultural significance of football are accessible, engaging, and seamlessly navigable for a global audience, encouraging an educational and immersive user experience.

Choice of Content and Model Website

Wikipedia (Association Football)

Description:

The Wikipedia article on Association Football covers subjects such as its historical evolution, core regulations, significant tournaments, and cultural impact across nations. It's notable for having a vast number of subsections that cover everything from gameplay and player responsibilities to international competitions and disputes, resulting in a dense informational structure.

Justification:

Given football's global appeal and complex nature, this article was chosen to provide a diverse range of rich, engaging information that might suit a diverse audience range. The large number of subsections ensures that there is enough content to build a well-rounded website that can serve as an informational hub on the subject.

Relevance:

Because of the article's complexity and depth, a website design that is both user-friendly and capable of rationally and attractively organizing, navigating, and presenting the content is required. It is vital to develop a platform that allows users to study the depths of football's history and explore the depths of football's background, making its subtle details accessible and interesting.
University of Ottawa

Description:

The website of the University of Ottawa stands out for its structured, clean, and intuitive design, which allows for easy navigation despite the range of information and services provided to various user types - students, staff, and visitors. It efficiently blends detailed content with simplicity and user centrality, thanks to a well-organized menu, cohesive layout, user-focused features, and modern design.

Justification:

This website was chosen as the model because of its shown capacity to manage, categorize, and present a wide range of information in an accessible and user-friendly manner. Its design is structured but not excessively complex, allowing users to obtain required information with minimal effort, which is critical when dealing with a topic as information-dense as football.

Compatibility:

The organizational structure and visual aesthetics of the University of Ottawa website provide an appropriate model for dealing with the considerable content from the Wikipedia page. The university website's categorical segmentation and navigational ease will be used to build a platform where football's diverse information is easily accessible, engagingly presented, and easy to navigate, ensuring an optimal user experience that balances information provision with aesthetic appeal.

My Process of Creaing IA:
1.I began by thoroughly analyzing the existing Wikipedia structure, which allowed me to understand its breadth and depth. This can be seen in the "Wikipedia Original IA", a detailed mapping of themes and subtopics linked to association football. 2.After reviewing the information, I highlighted key areas such as "Overview," "Fundamental," "Equipment and Field," and others. These are critical points of entry that provide users with a broad overview of the subject. 3.Layering Through Sub-Categorization: To guarantee users can delve further into specific areas, I arranged related topics beneath each core category, providing for intuitive and user-centric layered navigation.
4.Enhancement to Utility Navigation: In my revised IA, I prioritized utility navigation by emphasizing frequently-used actions like "Article," "Talk," and "Login" for simple access. 5.EAssociative Navigation Placement: Strategically placed footer navigation provides additional platform-related resources. While these are secondary in nature, they are critical for a complete user experience. 6.Streamlining for Clarity: I realized the value of a concise navigation system. I ensured that the IA remained uncluttered by revisiting and revising categories, as seen in the "Wikipedia Refined IA", resulting in a lower cognitive load for consumers.

Wireframes

Low-Fidelity Wireframes (Sketches) After completing the Information Architecture (IA), I immediately ventured into creating low-fidelity wireframes. These wireframes allowed me to quickly conceptualize the basic layout and flow of the site, ensuring it adhered to the guidelines and objectives of the IA.
Mid-Fidelity Wireframes Transitioning from the sketches, I dove into mid-fidelity wireframes. This step offered a more detailed view, bringing clarity to the user interface and interactions, acting as a bridge to the eventual high-fidelity design and development phase.

Development

Dreamweaver: The main tool for coding. Utilizing Dreamweaver, I was able to seamlessly integrate HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, benefiting from its intuitive interface and features which optimized the coding process.
Javascript: Introduced dynamic flair to the site. JavaScript facilitated interactive features like dropdown menus and sliders, enriching the user's interaction.
HTML: Employed as the backbone of the site, it structured our content from headings to images, creating an organized content hierarchy influenced by our Information Architecture.
CSS: This was the design's heartbeat. With CSS, the fundamental structure was aesthetically enhanced to match the design elements of the chosen Canadian university website.

Reflection

The CCT360 project required a combination of building vision, technological precision, and adaptability. I carefully formatted the Wikipedia information in HTML to ensure cohesion and logical flow. I wanted to recreate the design essence of my chosen Canadian university website with CSS, achieving a balance between its academic style and the dynamic nature of Wikipedia information.

There were couple of challenges throughout this project. First, is integrating Javascript to enhance the site interactivity. I had to navigate issues such as perfecting transitions and maintaining responsive layouts. Maintaining code consistency grew increasingly important as the volume of code expanded. It was just as important to keep the code clean, orderly, and legible as it was to keep it functioning. Another difficult issues was combining the Information Architecture (IA) of another website with the IA of the selected Wikipedia content. Merging two distinct organizational structures demanded a deep understanding of both, as well as a vision for an unified user experience. This integration process demonstrated the delicate balance of content strategy and user-centric design. Lastly, time was also an important factor. Balancing technical complexities with project deadlines required strategic planning and more coding knowledge. Furthermore, due to some time constraints, I wans't able to add things such as animation, parallex effects, and other things that will enhance the overall website.

This project was about integration, adaptability, and consistency, not just coding or design. The lessons learned from the project emphasized the significance of integrating design and development while maintaining a cohesive and coherent user experience. The website is still underdevelopment, so I will definetly keep implementing more changes to the website, design, and code.
